DWG 01

Tire Curing Molds & Bladder Tooling

MICHELIN (TIGAR TYRES) · 2020–2023 · SIEMENS NX

Passenger-car tire molds (14"–20")

Built fully parametric master models of tire curing molds in Siemens NX. Each mold family was driven by expression tables, so a single master model generated the complete 14"–20" size range from a spreadsheet-linked parameter set. Managed configuration and revision control across shared and size-specific components — first in Autodesk Vault, then leading the migration to Teamcenter PLM.

Curing bladder tooling

The bladder tooling — used to form the butyl-rubber curing bladder — is structurally more complex than the tire molds themselves and had historically been designed as 2D AutoCAD drawings. I moved this workflow into NX using a top-down parametric assembly driven by a controlling skeleton, which noticeably shortened the design cycle for each new tooling variant.

DWG 02

Personalized Prosthetic Leg for a Dog

UNIVERSITY OF NIŠ · 2019 · REVERSE ENGINEERING + 3D PRINT

Team project to design and build an external, easily fitted prosthesis for a dog (Adonis) that had lost part of a hind leg — chosen over an implant to avoid additional surgery.

Reverse engineering the stump

The residual limb was captured with a dental impression compound and cast in wax. Because the undercut surfaces couldn't be 3D-scanned in one pass, the positive was split into two lacquer-coated halves, scanned separately, and reverse-engineered in CAD: point cloud → polygon mesh → surface → solid reference geometry.

Two-material design

A flexible NinjaFlex upper socket grips the stump and flexes with movement; a rigid PLA lower foot mates precisely with it for durability. The fit, clamping, neck angle and ground-contact shape were refined over multiple fitting rounds on the dog itself.

DWG 03

Robotic Machine-Tending Cell

UNIVERSITY OF NIŠ · 2020 · ABB ROBOTSTUDIO + RAPID

Concept, build and simulation of a robotic cell that loads and unloads a CNC lathe. A Ø50 × 180 mm steel part arrives on a 3000 × 400 mm roller conveyor; a sensor signals the pickup, the robot loads the chuck, retracts during machining, then places the finished part in a collection bin.

My contribution covered robot and gripper selection, building and simulating the station in RobotStudio, and the basic I/O signal logic between robot, gripper and machine — including a working RAPID control program delivered with a full simulation video.

The write-up also identifies concrete improvements: moving door/chuck logic onto the CNC's own controller via M-code outputs, and replacing fixed wait-times in RAPID with real machine handshake signals.

DWG 05

FEA: Static, Thermal & Optimization Study

MASTER'S COURSEWORK · ANSYS WORKBENCH

Static structural

Half-symmetry model of a bolted bracket with frictionless supports replacing removed screws; "No Separation" contact kept the solver linear while still permitting small slip. Automatic mesh refinement (5% convergence criterion) stabilized after four passes: safety factor 1.52 against yield vs. a 1.25 target — a clear optimization candidate.

Thermo-structural coupling

Steady-state thermal load (120 °C at the bolt face, natural convection, 23 °C ambient) fed into a duplicated structural run. The combined loading pushed the countersunk-hole region to roughly 4× yield — proving the part needed a design change (lower-conductivity material, different joint, or a relief groove) to survive its thermal duty cycle.

DWG 06

Master's Thesis: RTM Mold Tooling for a CT-Scanner Panel

UNIVERSITY OF NIŠ · CAD → CAM → MACHINED MOLD SET

Full path from a composite housing panel's CAD model to a machined, ready-to-use RTM mold set: part simplification for tooling, female + segmented male mold design, and CNC machining of both halves from MDF on a 3-axis router.

Beating the undercut

The panel's wrap-around shape creates a global undercut that would lock a one-piece male tool inside the part — so the male form was split into nine segments, each seated at a 5° draft against its neighbors on a common mounting plate, with every parting line on a straight edge for clean extraction after each shot.

Machining & cutting data

The ~1.1 × 1.0 m female cavity was machined in two setups with toe clamps; down-cut carbide router bits (Ø20/Ø12.7/Ø5) plus a Ø6 ball-nose for profiled surfaces, 10,000–18,000 rpm, finishing stepovers down to 0.01–0.2 mm on the A-surface that directly shapes the finished composite part.
